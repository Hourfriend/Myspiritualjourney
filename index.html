Some people move through life collecting experiences that fit neatly inside what they already believe. I have had a different kind of life.

Not dramatic. Not chosen. Just strange in ways that left marks.

Over the years, a series of moments — quiet, overwhelming, terrifying, beautiful — pushed me past the edges of ordinary explanation. I did not go looking for most of them. But each one asked something of me: to stay curious, to resist easy answers, to hold the feeling of not knowing and remain present in it anyway.

This is a record of those moments. Not proof of anything. Just the shape of how I got here.

Formative Experiences · 2008 – 2022
A Reflection

These experiences did not make me certain of anything. If anything, they made me less certain — and more at peace with that.

I am not someone who walks around waiting for the next sign. I live an ordinary life most of the time. I make coffee. I watch the sky change. I laugh easily. But underneath all of that, there is a layer of the world I cannot unsee — a dimension of experience that feels older and stranger and more interested in us than we tend to assume.

I am not trying to convince you of anything. I just thought you should know this about me. Because it shapes everything: how I listen, how I love, what I am drawn to, and how I show up when things get quietly strange.

Who This Resonates With

The person who finds the
unknown interesting.

I do not need someone who has had the same experiences. I need someone who can hold mystery without collapsing it — who can sit with a strange feeling and let it be strange.

Someone emotionally deep enough to go somewhere real in a conversation. Curious enough to wonder what they do not yet know about themselves. Comfortable with the fact that not everything is explainable, and that this is not a problem to be solved.

If any of this felt familiar — or even just intriguing — I suspect we would have something worth exploring.
